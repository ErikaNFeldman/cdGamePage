Make a child below to model how child development unfolds.

We've created a model of early childhood development that uses a 0 to 200 point scale to represent where early learners may be "in a few years" when they enter middle childhood between the ages of 7 and 10. Each child develops differently and maturational age is just one factor that determines when they reach a point where all there competencies congeal into a set framework that places them within a new developmental stage. Don't worry: they all make it to middle childhood. However, some are better positioned when they get there.

Press here to see where our kids might be on their developmental trajectory in a few years.

As you will see in our form options, we've scoped our model to include children who attend early learning and care environments (including centers and family child care) on a full time basis. On a National level, states are creating rating systems for the quality of early learning and care environments in order to make quality more transparent to families. Here we've used the five point rating system used in Early Acheivers - Washington State's Quality Rating and Improvement System(Early Achivers - WA QRIS). We've created pretend ratings for our pretend children so that you can play around with the potential impact of the quality of early learning and care environments along with other environmental and internal factors that impact child development. **Please note: Our model is not comprehensive (e.g., it doesn't explicitly measure nutrition and healthcare for each child).

Make Your Child:

"Child Development is a dynamic interplay of factors unfolding within each child and between the child and their environment." This page models how a child's compentencies in different domains (e.g., Social and Emotional Development) are impacted by environmental factors (e.g., number of siblings) and interact with eachother to influence the child's developmental level a little bit later in life. Think of developmental level as seperate from age and as a more abstract way to represent a child's current ability. See how changing different factors (e.g., amount of time spent passively engaging with media) impacts how quickly your child develops.

The best way to think about the resutls of our model is in terms of where our children will place on their developmental trajectory in a few years. We can't say for sure that our point system has an absolute zero or that the increments between points are always the same.
